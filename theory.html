1. Ohm's Law

Definition: Ohm's Law states that the current flowing through a conductor is directly proportional to the voltage across it and inversely proportional to its resistance.

Formulas:

  • V = I × R
  • I = V / R
  • R = V / I

Where: V = Voltage (Volts, V), I = Current (Amperes, A), R = Resistance (Ohms, Ω)

Example: A resistor of 10 Ω has a current of 2 A. Voltage: V = I × R = 2 × 10 = 20 V

2. Joule's Law

Definition: Joule's Law states that the heat produced in a conductor by an electric current is proportional to the square of the current, the resistance, and the time for which the current flows.

Formulas:

  • H = I² × R × t
  • H = V × I × t
  • H = V² / R × t

Where: H = Heat energy (Joules, J), I = Current (Amperes, A), R = Resistance (Ohms, Ω), t = Time (seconds, s), V = Voltage (Volts, V)

Example: Current I = 3 A, R = 5 Ω, t = 10 s. Heat: H = 3² × 5 × 10 = 450 J

3. Electric Power

Definition: Electric power is the rate at which electrical energy is consumed or produced in a circuit.

Formulas:

  • P = E / t
  • P = V × I
  • P = I² × R
  • P = V² / R

Where: P = Power (Watts, W), E = Energy (Joules, J), t = Time (s), V = Voltage (V), I = Current (A), R = Resistance (Ω)

Example: Device with I = 12 A, V = 240 V. Power: P = V × I = 240 × 12 = 2880 W = 2.88 kW. Energy if run 3 hours: E = P × t = 2.88 × 3 = 8.64 kWh

4. Series Circuit

Definition: A series circuit has all components connected end-to-end in a single path. The same current flows through all components.

Characteristics:

  • Current: I_total = I1 = I2 = …
  • Voltage: V_total = V1 + V2 + …
  • Resistance: R_total = R1 + R2 + …
  • Power: P_total = P1 + P2 + …
Example: R1=2Ω, R2=3Ω, R3=5Ω, V=20V. Total Resistance: R_total = 10Ω. Current: I = 20 / 10 = 2 A. Voltages: V1=4V, V2=6V, V3=10V

5. Parallel Circuit

Definition: A parallel circuit has components connected across the same two points, creating multiple paths for current.

Characteristics:

  • Voltage: V_total = V1 = V2 = …
  • Current: I_total = I1 + I2 + …
  • Resistance: 1 / R_total = 1 / R1 + 1 / R2 + …
  • Power: P_total = P1 + P2 + …
Example: R1=6Ω, R2=3Ω, R3=2Ω, V=12V. 1/R_total = 1/6 + 1/3 + 1/2 = 1 → R_total = 1Ω. Currents: I1=2A, I2=4A, I3=6A, I_total=12A
